### 1. Executive Summary

This Diagnostic Defect Report synthesizes findings from a thermal imaging inspection and a visual building inspection of Flat No. 103. The property exhibits widespread and critical water-related defects, severely impacting its condition. The visual inspection reveals pervasive dampness at skirting levels in multiple rooms, extensive issues with bathroom waterproofing (tile gaps, hollowness, concealed plumbing leaks, Nahani trap damage), compromised external building envelope (moderate cracks, leaking external plumbing), and active leaks into common areas (parking ceiling below Flat No. 103) and from an upper unit (Flat No. 203).

A critical structural concern involves moderate cracks observed on RCC Columns and Beams, which contradicts an initial 100% structural condition rating and necessitates immediate expert structural assessment. The thermal inspection, however, identified only minor, low-severity thermal anomalies (consistent ~5°C differentials) which do not directly correlate with the severity or specific locations of the identified water ingress and structural issues. The primary defects are related to water management and structural integrity, demanding urgent and comprehensive corrective actions to prevent further degradation, address safety risks, and restore the property's habitability.

### 2. Property Condition Overview

Flat No. 103 is currently in **fair to poor condition**, primarily due to chronic and widespread water ingress. The property is afflicted by significant skirting-level dampness across the Hall, Common Bedroom, Master Bedroom, and Kitchen. Internal bathrooms (Common and Master Bedroom bathrooms in Flat 103) are major sources of these issues, exhibiting defective tile joints, tile hollowness, and confirmed concealed plumbing and Nahani trap failures. These defects have led to **active leakage at the parking ceiling directly below Flat No. 103**.

Further contributing to water ingress are deficiencies in the external building envelope, including moderate cracks on external walls, a noted duct issue, and cracked, leaking external plumbing pipes near the Master Bedroom. The presence of efflorescence on the Master Bedroom wall confirms prolonged moisture exposure. Additionally, leakage from Flat No. 203 (above Flat No. 103) is causing dampness in Flat No. 103's Common Bathroom ceiling, stemming from similar tile joint gaps and outlet leakages in the upper unit's bathrooms.

A serious structural concern has been identified with moderate cracks on **RCC Columns and Beams**. This finding is of paramount importance and overrides any general positive structural assessment, indicating a potential compromise to the building's structural integrity. The pervasive water damage creates conditions ripe for mold growth, further material degradation, and potential safety hazards. Without immediate and comprehensive intervention, the condition of Flat No. 103 is expected to deteriorate further.

### 3. Thermal Findings Summary

The thermal inspection, conducted on September 27, 2022, across 30 distinct areas, identified consistent but relatively minor thermal anomalies. Hotspot temperatures ranged from 25.1 °C to 28.8 °C, and coldspot temperatures ranged from 20.1 °C to 23.4 °C. The reflected (ambient) temperature was consistently 23 °C. A uniform temperature differential (Hotspot - Coldspot) of approximately 5.0 °C was observed across almost all images.

The highest recorded hotspot was 5.8 °C above ambient, and the lowest coldspot was 2.9 °C below ambient. These findings suggest slight, consistent thermal variations rather than acute failures. The overall severity rating for the thermal anomalies is **Low**, indicating minor thermal inefficiencies or standard operational variations within the building's envelope, rather than critical issues like severe electrical overheating, major moisture intrusion, or significant energy loss requiring urgent attention.

### 5. Correlated Issues

A direct correlation between the thermal findings and the specific, severe water ingress issues identified by the visual inspection is **not evident**.

*   **Thermal Findings (Low Severity):** The thermal report indicates minor thermal inefficiencies with consistent ~5°C temperature differentials. These are typical for minor insulation gaps, slight air leakage, or general building envelope inconsistencies, and do not suggest severe, active water intrusion. Water-saturated materials or active evaporative cooling from significant leaks would typically manifest as much colder spots, often with larger temperature differentials, which were not observed or reported.
*   **Visual Inspection Findings (High Severity):** The visual inspection clearly identifies pervasive dampness, efflorescence, and active, continuous water leaks from multiple sources (bathrooms, external walls, concealed plumbing) into Flat No. 103 and the parking area below.

**Conclusion on Correlation:** The thermal data does not provide direct supporting evidence for the severe water ingress detected visually. This suggests that:
1.  The thermal inspection might not have been performed in conditions optimal for detecting active moisture (e.g., insufficient temperature differential between interior and exterior, or inadequate drying cycle post-leak).
2.  The thermal images provided may not have focused on the precise locations of severe water ingress, as specific locations were "Not specified."
3.  The type of water ingress (e.g., slow capillary action, concealed leaks not affecting surface temperature significantly) may not have created detectable thermal signatures with the parameters used, or the surface materials might have obscured clear thermal patterns.
The low-severity thermal anomalies found may relate to general heat loss/gain characteristics of the building envelope, independent of the active water leaks.

### 7. Root Cause Analysis

The overarching root cause of the current property defects is **systemic and widespread water ingress** originating from multiple compromised areas:

1.  **Defective Wet Area Waterproofing and Plumbing:** The primary source of internal dampness and active leaks stems from failures within the Common and Master Bedroom bathrooms of Flat No. 103, and the bathrooms of Flat No. 203 above. This includes:
    *   **Poorly maintained or installed tile joints and hollow tiles:** Allowing surface water to penetrate the subfloor.
    *   **Concealed plumbing defects:** Undetected leaks within wall or floor cavities.
    *   **Damaged Nahani traps/Brickbat coba:** Failure of critical waterproofing and drainage components in wet areas.
    *   **Loose plumbing joints/rust:** Indicating deterioration of plumbing infrastructure.
    These issues lead directly to skirting dampness, efflorescence, and active leaks into the parking ceiling and the ceiling of Flat No. 103.

2.  **Compromised External Building Envelope:**
    *   **Moderate cracks on external walls:** Allowing rainwater penetration.
    *   **Cracked and leaking external plumbing pipes:** Direct external source of water contributing to wall dampness and potentially migrating internally.
    *   **Duct issue:** A breach in the building envelope allowing water entry.
    These external deficiencies contribute to internal dampness and prolonged moisture exposure evident by algae/fungus growth.

3.  **Potential Structural Deficiency (RCC Members):** The cracks on RCC Column and Beam could be aggravated by long-term water ingress, or they might indicate underlying structural design or construction flaws. Without further assessment, the precise root cause cannot be fully determined, but it represents a critical concern.

### 8. Recommended Corrective Actions

**Immediate Actions (Within 0-7 days):**

1.  **Structural Assessment:** Engage a qualified structural engineer to immediately assess the moderate cracks on the RCC Columns and Beams to determine their cause, extent, and impact on structural integrity.
2.  **Stop Active Leaks:** Immediately identify and stop all active water leaks, especially those affecting the parking ceiling below Flat No. 103 and the Common Bathroom ceiling of Flat No. 103. This will likely involve isolating water supply to affected bathrooms temporarily.

**Urgent Actions (Within 1-4 weeks):**

3.  **Comprehensive Bathroom Repairs (Flat No. 103):**
    *   Expose and repair all concealed plumbing leaks in the Common and Master Bedroom Bathrooms.
    *   Repair or replace damaged Nahani traps and Brickbat coba.
    *   Re-grout all tile joints and re-lay any hollow tiles.
    *   Inspect and address all loose/rusted plumbing joints.
    *   Ensure proper waterproofing and drainage for all wet areas.
4.  **Bathroom Repairs (Flat No. 203):** Coordinate with Flat No. 203 to address gaps between tile joints and outlet leakages in their Common and Master Bedroom Bathrooms to prevent further damage to Flat No. 103.
5.  **External Plumbing Repair:** Repair or replace all cracked and leaking external plumbing pipes.
6.  **External Wall Repair:** Repair all moderate cracks on the external wall and address the identified duct issue to restore the building envelope's integrity.
7.  **Dampness Remediation:** Investigate the precise sources of all skirting-level dampness in the Hall, Common Bedroom, Master Bedroom, and Kitchen. Dry out affected areas thoroughly, treat for mold if present, and then repair and re-paint damaged surfaces after all water sources are definitively resolved. Address efflorescence on the Master Bedroom wall, which will require scraping and potentially a salt-resistant primer.

**Long-term Actions (Within 1-3 months, pending structural assessment):**

8.  **Post-Structural Repair:** Implement all recommendations from the structural engineer, which may include strengthening or repair of RCC members.
9.  **External Wall Maintenance:** Clean external walls of algae, fungus, and moss. Consider applying appropriate exterior treatments or paint with anti-fungal properties.
10. **Energy Efficiency Audit:** While low severity, an energy audit could be considered to address minor thermal inconsistencies identified by the thermal imaging for potential energy savings.
